Frequently Asked Questions

And Answers


Isn’t it dangerous to hike in the Caucasus?

Hiking in the Caucasus entails many of the same risks that hikers find anywhere. In addition, some parts of the TCT travel through areas that require knowledge and experience traveling in high-alpine environments. If you don’t have that experience, we recommend hiring a guide. In general, the security situation in the mountains south of the Russian border has improved greatly over the past decade, but that situation can change quickly. It is always advisable to check any government travel warnings.


Can I just wander across the borders in the mountains?

No. Only cross borders at official crossings. Crossing borders anywhere else is a good way to end up in jail, or worse. Some maps for planned trails on this website do not show the recognized border crossings. Also, some borders in the region are not recognized or agreed upon by all parties. It is your responsibility to make sure you are not illegally crossing any borders. In the future, we will have more resources on this website for planning trips in the region.


Can you actually hike the whole TCT?

Right now, several hundred kilometers of trail exist. There are spectacular treks through the regions of Svaneti, Racha, Mtianeti, Khevsureti, Tusheti, Lagodekhi National Park, and Borjomi National Park. Trying to connect any of these established routes is not advisable at this point.


How will you build a trail in a region with breakaway territories and frozen conflicts?

It won’t be easy, and it may take many years, but we believe it is possible. People have crossed these mountains for centuries, and we hope it will become easier to cross certain areas in the decades ahead. It may be wishful thinking, but we hope that by starting to build a trail, we will help to spur more wishful thinking. In the meantime, there are hundreds of kilometers of trail to hike. See you in the mountains!
